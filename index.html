Second & Third Conditional
Aprende a hablar de situaciones hipotéticas, imaginarias y pasadas. Esencial para conversaciones reales a nivel B2.
👁️ Reading · Lectura
B2 · Upper-Intermediate · Reading

The Road Not Taken

Last year, Maya turned down a job offer in London. At the time, she thought it was the right decision — she didn't want to leave her family. But now, a year later, she sometimes wonders: "If I took that job, where would I be now?"

Her friend Carlos has the opposite problem. He did move to another city for work three years ago, and he regrets it deeply. "If I hadn't moved," he says, "I would have stayed close to my parents. I would have been happier."

Both Maya and Carlos are using conditionals — but in different ways. Maya is talking about an imaginary present situation: she didn't take the job, but she's imagining a different reality now. Carlos is talking about an impossible past: he can't undo what happened, but he's reflecting on it.

These two types of conditional sentences — the Second and Third Conditional — are among the most important structures in English for expressing hypothetical ideas, wishes, and regrets.

📌 Vocabulario clave del texto
turn downrechazar una oferta
wonderpreguntarse / imaginar
regretarrepentirse
undodeshacer algo del pasado
hypotheticalhipotético / imaginario
reflect onreflexionar sobre algo

👂 Listening · Diálogo
Situación: Dos amigos hablando sobre sus decisiones de vida
A
Do you ever regret not studying abroad?
¿Alguna vez te arrepientes de no haber estudiado en el extranjero?
B
Sometimes. If I had studied abroad, I would have learned English much faster.
A veces. Si hubiera estudiado en el extranjero, habría aprendido inglés mucho más rápido.
A
I know the feeling. What would you do now if you had more free time?
Entiendo esa sensación. ¿Qué harías ahora si tuvieras más tiempo libre?
B
If I had more free time, I would definitely travel to Europe.
Si tuviera más tiempo libre, definitivamente viajaría a Europa.
A
Me too! And if I were you, I wouldn't wait. Life is too short.
¡Yo también! Y si fuera tú, no esperaría. La vida es muy corta.
B
You're right. If I hadn't spent so much money last year, I would have saved enough by now.
Tienes razón. Si no hubiera gastado tanto dinero el año pasado, ya habría ahorrado suficiente.
A
Well, it's not too late. If you started saving now, you could travel by next summer!
Bueno, no es demasiado tarde. Si empezaras a ahorrar ahora, ¡podrías viajar para el próximo verano!
🎯 Identifica los condicionales en el diálogo

¿Cuál es 2nd Conditional (presente/futuro imaginario) y cuál es 3rd Conditional (pasado imposible)?

🗣️ Tu turno: Escucha o lee el diálogo otra vez y subraya todas las frases con conditional. ¿Puedes notar la diferencia entre "would + base verb" y "would + have + past participle"?
✍️ Grammar · Gramática
2nd Conditional — Situaciones hipotéticas presentes/futuras
¿Cuándo se usa?
Hablas de algo imaginario, irreal o muy poco probable en el presente o futuro. La situación NO es real ahora. Ejemplo: no tienes un millón de dólares, pero imaginas qué harías si lo tuvieras.
ParteEstructuraEjemplo
IF clauseIf + Past SimpleIf I had more money...
Main clausewould / could / might + base verb...I would travel the world.
✦ Ejemplos completos
If I were you, I wouldn't wait.
Si fuera tú, no esperaría.
If she spoke English, she could apply for that job.
Si hablara inglés, podría solicitar ese trabajo.
What would you do if you had a million dollars?
¿Qué harías si tuvieras un millón de dólares?
If it weren't raining, we would go to the park.
Si no lloviera, iríamos al parque.
⚠️ Nota importante: "If I were"
En el 2nd Conditional, con el verbo to be, siempre se usa "were" (no "was") — para todas las personas: I were, he were, she were.
✓ "If I were rich..." (correcto)  ·  ✗ "If I was rich..." (informal/incorrecto en B2)
3rd Conditional — Situaciones pasadas imposibles / arrepentimientos
¿Cuándo se usa?
Hablas de algo que pasó en el pasado (o no pasó), y ahora imaginas cómo habrían sido las cosas de forma diferente. Es imposible cambiar lo que ya ocurrió. Se usa mucho para expresar arrepentimientos.
ParteEstructuraEjemplo
IF clauseIf + Past Perfect (had + pp)If I had studied more...
Main clausewould / could / might + have + past participle...I would have passed the exam.
✦ Ejemplos completos
If I had studied more, I would have passed.
Si hubiera estudiado más, habría aprobado.
If she hadn't left early, she would have met him.
Si no hubiera salido temprano, lo habría conocido.
They could have won if they had practiced more.
Podrían haber ganado si hubieran practicado más.
If he had taken the medicine, he might have recovered faster.
Si hubiera tomado la medicina, quizás se habría recuperado más rápido.
⚡ Comparación rápida: 2nd vs 3rd Conditional
2nd Conditional3rd Conditional
TiempoPresente / futuro imaginarioPasado — ya no puede cambiar
IF + verboPast Simple (had, went, spoke...)Past Perfect (had had, had gone...)
Main clausewould/could + base verbwould/could + have + past participle
EjemploIf I lived in NY, I would...If I had lived in NY, I would have...
SensaciónImaginación / deseo actualArrepentimiento / especulación pasada
🌟 Bonus B2: Mixed Conditional
Nivel extra
A veces combinamos tiempo pasado con resultado presente. Esto es el Mixed Conditional — muy común en inglés avanzado.
IF clauseMain clauseSignificado
If + Past Perfectwould + base verbPasado → efecto en el presente
If I had studied medicine, I would be a doctor now.Si hubiera estudiado medicina, sería médico ahora.
If she hadn't moved away, she would still be my neighbor.Si no se hubiera mudado, todavía sería mi vecina.

Advanced Passive Voice
Domina todas las formas de la voz pasiva en inglés: tiempos compuestos, causativa, pasiva con modales y estructuras formales de nivel B2.
👁️ Reading · Lectura
B2 · Upper-Intermediate · Passive Voice

Behind the Scenes

Every year, thousands of films are produced in Hollywood. By the time a movie hits the screens, it has been edited dozens of times. Some scenes are being reshot even as you read this.

The script was written by a team of writers, and the final version had been approved by the studio before filming began. The actors were told to memorize their lines three weeks in advance.

The special effects will have been completed before the premiere. The film is expected to be released in December. It is said to be the most expensive production of the decade.

The director had the entire set redesigned three times. She also had the soundtrack recorded by a live orchestra — a detail that has been praised by critics worldwide.

🎯 Identifica la forma pasiva

Para cada oración del diálogo en negrita, escribe qué tiempo verbal es.

✍️ Grammar · Gramática
📊 Todas las formas pasivas — tabla completa
TiempoEstructura pasivaEjemplo
Present Simpleam/is/are + ppThe report is written weekly.
Present Continuousam/is/are being + ppThe report is being written now.
Present Perfecthas/have been + ppThe report has been written.
Past Simplewas/were + ppThe report was written yesterday.
Past Continuouswas/were being + ppThe report was being written all day.
Past Perfecthad been + ppThe report had been written before I arrived.
Future Simplewill be + ppThe report will be written tomorrow.
Future Perfectwill have been + ppThe report will have been written by Friday.
Modal Passivemodal + be + ppThe report must be written carefully.
🧠 Regla universal
La fórmula siempre es: forma de "to be" + participio pasado. El tiempo se expresa en el verbo "to be", nunca en el participio.
🔧 Pasiva Causativa — Have/Get something done
¿Cuándo se usa?
Cuando mandas a alguien a hacer algo por ti — un servicio, trabajo o tarea. No lo haces tú, alguien más lo hace para ti.
EstructuraEjemploEspañol
have + object + past participleI had my hair cut.Me corté el cabello (lo hizo alguien).
get + object + past participleShe got her car fixed.Mandó arreglar su carro.
PasadoWe had the office painted.Mandamos pintar la oficina.
FuturoI'm going to have my phone repaired.Voy a mandar arreglar mi teléfono.
NegativoI didn't have it delivered.No lo mandé entregar.
✦ Más ejemplos cotidianos
I need to have my eyes tested.
Necesito hacerme un examen de la vista.
She's having her apartment redecorated.
Está mandando redecorar su departamento.
We got the documents signed yesterday.
Mandamos firmar los documentos ayer.
You should get your laptop checked.
Deberías mandar revisar tu laptop.
📰 Pasiva con verbos de reporte — It is said / expected / believed
¿Cuándo se usa?
Muy común en noticias, reportes y textos formales. Se usa cuando la fuente es desconocida o cuando se habla de opinión general.
EstructuraEjemploEspañol
It is said that…It is said that she resigned.Se dice que renunció.
It is believed that…It is believed that prices will rise.Se cree que los precios subirán.
It is expected that…It is expected that results improve.Se espera que los resultados mejoren.
It is reported that…It is reported that 100 jobs were lost.Se reporta que se perdieron 100 empleos.
Subject + is said to…She is said to be very talented.Se dice que es muy talentosa.
Subject + is expected to…Prices are expected to rise.Se espera que los precios suban.
💡 Dos estructuras equivalentes
"People say she is brilliant." →
✅ "It is said that she is brilliant."
✅ "She is said to be brilliant."
Ambas son correctas y muy naturales en inglés escrito y formal.
⚙️ Pasiva con verbos modales
Modal + be + ppEjemploEspañol
must be + ppThis form must be signed.Este formulario debe ser firmado.
should be + ppThe report should be submitted today.El reporte debería entregarse hoy.
can be + ppThis problem can be solved.Este problema puede resolverse.
could be + ppMore jobs could be created.Podrían crearse más empleos.
might be + ppThe event might be cancelled.El evento podría ser cancelado.
should have been + ppIt should have been done earlier.Debería haberse hecho antes.
